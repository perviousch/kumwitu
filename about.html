About the Movement

Our Home, Our Future

Kumwitu Uku Mwitu means "Our Home, Our Future." It represents a belief that development must start with the people who live, work, and dream within the community. Rooted in Mbala, the movement focuses on youth empowerment, unity, and opportunity creation.

Our Mission

To empower young people by connecting them to opportunities, knowledge, financial growth, and mobility through a supportive community-driven platform.

Our Vision

A united, self-reliant generation of youth shaping Mbala's future through innovation, responsibility, and collective action.

Core Values

Empowerment

Enabling youth to reach their full potential

Unity

Together we build stronger communities

Innovation

Creative solutions for community challenges

Responsibility

Accountable action for positive change

Community Impact

Measurable positive outcomes for Mbala

Mbala District: Key History

1890s

British Colonial Establishment

Mbala (then known as Abercorn) was established as a British colonial outpost in Northern Rhodesia, serving as an administrative center and trading post in the region.

1918

End of World War I

The signing of the Armistice that ended World War I was announced in Mbala, making it historically significant as one of the first places in Africa to receive the news.

1964

Zambian Independence

With Zambia's independence, Mbala became part of the new nation, transitioning from colonial administration to local governance and self-determination.

1970s-1980s

Regional Development

Mbala District saw infrastructure development including roads, schools, and healthcare facilities, strengthening its role as a key district in Northern Province.

2000s-Present

Community Empowerment Era

Modern Mbala focuses on youth development, economic empowerment, and community-led initiatives like Kumwitu Uku Mwitu, building on its rich heritage for a brighter future.
